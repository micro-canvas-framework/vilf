# Annex 01 — Terms of Reference (ToR) — Innovation Labs (Tier-0)

## 01.1 Purpose
This ToR defines the mandate, scope, governance alignment, roles, responsibilities, and evidence requirements for **Innovation Labs (Tier-0)** operating under the Vigía Innovation Lab Framework (VILF 1.0).

Tier-0 labs are the foundational execution units of the VILF ecosystem. Their mandate is to generate validated evidence, test solutions, collaborate across hubs, and support institutional or national innovation objectives through structured innovation cycles.

## 01.2 Scope
This ToR applies to any Innovation Lab participating in a VILF-compliant network, including:

- Government innovation labs
- University or academic labs
- Corporate labs participating in public–private ecosystems
- NGO or civil-society innovation labs

## 01.3 Mandate
Tier-0 labs must:

- Execute projects using **IMM-P® Six-Stage Cycle**
- Maintain complete **evidence repositories**
- Follow **IGF governance** rules
- Participate in **MEL reviews and scorecard cycles**
- Contribute to system-wide learning
- Uphold documentation integrity and transparency

## 01.4 Responsibilities

### Execution Responsibilities
- Conduct Pre-Discovery, Discovery, Validation, Efficiency, Scaling, and Continuous Improvement using IMM-P®
- Produce all required evidence artifacts
- Facilitate user research, prototyping, and experimentation
- Document decisions, assumptions, risks, and governance escalations

### Governance Responsibilities
- Adhere to Tier-0 governance mandates
- Escalate risks above lab authority to hubs (Tier-1)
- Implement IGF decision-pathway rules

### MEL Responsibilities
- Submit quarterly KPI data
- Participate in MEL reviews
- Support evidence audits

### Collaboration Responsibilities
- Contribute to cross-lab knowledge
- Participate in domain clusters
- Engage with hub-coordinated capability sessions

## 01.5 Lab Team Structure
Required minimum roles:

- Lab Manager
- Service Designer
- Experimentation Lead
- Evidence Officer
- Subject matter experts (as needed)

## 01.6 Outputs
- Completed IMM-P® evidence artifacts
- Prototyping results
- Experimentation logs
- MEL quarterly submissions
- Lab Scorecard
- Annual evidence repository

## 01.7 Version Compatibility
Compatible with VILF 1.0, VXF standards, IMM 2.1, IMM-P®, IGF, and MEL.

# Annex 02 — Terms of Reference (ToR) — Hubs (Tier-1)

## 02.1 Purpose
This ToR defines the mandate, governance alignment, capability responsibilities, and portfolio oversight for **Hubs (Tier-1)** in a VILF ecosystem.

Hubs serve as capability engines, cross-lab integrators, and the intermediate governance layer between labs and the Network Coordination Unit (NCU).

## 02.2 Scope
Applies to any VILF-aligned Hub, including:

- Sector hubs (health, education, mobility)
- Regional hubs
- Academic research hubs
- Public–private capability hubs

## 02.3 Mandate
Hubs must:

- Strengthen capabilities of multiple labs
- Provide portfolio governance and normalization
- Support MEL analysis and benchmarking
- Ensure documentation quality and repository hygiene
- Facilitate cross-lab learning and collaboration
- Enforce IGF Tier-1 governance rules

## 02.4 Responsibilities

### Capability Development Responsibilities
- Deliver IMM, MCF, futures, and experimentation training
- Provide coaching for lab managers and evidence officers
- Support institutional maturity progression

### Portfolio Governance Responsibilities
- Manage lab portfolios with balance and coherence
- Normalize KPI and MEL submissions
- Conduct cross-lab reviews and synthesis
- Detect capability and governance gaps

### Documentation Responsibilities
- Ensure evidence repository compliance
- Maintain domain-level documentation integrity

### Governance Responsibilities
- Enforce IGF decision pathways for Tier-1
- Escalate institutional risks to Tier-2 (NCU)

## 02.5 Hub Team Structure
Minimum roles:

- Hub Coordinator
- Capability Coaches
- Domain Specialists
- MEL Officers
- Documentation Officers (optional depending on size)

## 02.6 Outputs
- Portfolio reports
- Normalized KPI submissions
- Hub Scorecard
- Capability development plans
- Domain synthesis reports
- Quarterly MEL summaries

## 02.7 Version Compatibility
Compatible with VILF 1.0, VXF, IMM-P®, IGF Tier-1 rules, and MEL.

# Annex 03 — Terms of Reference (ToR) — Network Coordination Unit (Tier-2)

## 03.1 Purpose
This ToR establishes the mandate, governance authority, accreditation roles, documentation stewardship, and ecosystem-level accountability of the **Network Coordination Unit (NCU)**.

NCU is the apex governance body in a VILF network. It safeguards system integrity and ensures interoperability, transparency, and long-term sustainability.

## 03.2 Scope
Applies to:

- National NCU (for government-backed deployments)
- Multi-institutional NCU (universities, NGOs, public–private alliances)
- Regional or supranational NCU equivalents

## 03.3 Mandate
NCU must:

- Maintain and enforce IGF governance rules
- Oversee accreditation cycles for labs and hubs
- Safeguard documentation and evidence standards
- Lead national or multi-institutional MEL synthesis
- Manage benchmarking and performance reports
- Ensure transparency and accountability
- Provide authoritative guidance for scaling

## 03.4 Responsibilities

### Governance Responsibilities
- Enforce Tier-2 decision-rights
- Conduct audits and governance reviews
- Arbitrate escalations between hubs and labs
- Protect system integrity against political volatility

### Accreditation Responsibilities
- Accredit labs and hubs annually
- Maintain criteria and rubrics
- Ensure compliance with IMM-P®, MEL, and IGF

### MEL Responsibilities
- Consolidate national MEL reports
- Conduct benchmarking cycles
- Publish annual performance synthesis

### Documentation Responsibilities
- Maintain the evidence repository architecture
- Oversee metadata standards
- Ensure version control and public transparency (when applicable)

### Strategic Responsibilities
- Lead system expansion and roadmap execution
- Align VILF with national or institutional strategy
- Coordinate international cooperation

## 03.5 NCU Team Structure
Minimum roles:

- Network Director
- Standards & Governance Unit
- Learning & Performance Unit
- Infrastructure & Documentation Unit

## 03.6 Outputs
- National benchmarking report
- Annual MEL synthesis
- Accreditation decisions
- Governance audits
- System performance dashboard
- Documentation integrity reports

## 03.7 Version Compatibility
Compatible with VILF 1.0, VXF, MCF 2.1, IMM, IMM-P®, IGF, and MEL.

| Decision Domain | Tier-0 (Labs) | Tier-1 (Hubs) | Tier-2 (NCU) |
|-----------------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Research design | Decide | Review | Oversee |
| Experimentation | Decide | Review | Oversee |
| IMM-P® evidence | Produce | Validate | Audit |
| Documentation | Produce | Enforce quality | Govern standards |
| MEL submission | Submit | Normalize | Synthesize |
| Portfolio alignment | Inform | Decide | Oversee |
| Governance escalation | Initiate | Handle | Arbitrate |
| Accreditation | Participate | Participate | Decide |
| Funding eligibility | Input | Recommend | Decide |
| Risk management | Identify | Review | Govern |

## 05.4 Usage  
This matrix must accompany:
- all governance protocols  
- MEL reviews  
- accreditation cycles  
- NCU audits

# Annex 07 — Documentation & Evidence Standards

## 07.1 Purpose
This annex defines the standardized documentation and evidence requirements for all VILF-compliant labs, hubs, and the Network Coordination Unit (NCU).  
It ensures documentation integrity, auditability, transparency, and interoperability across the innovation ecosystem.

## 07.2 Evidence Principles
All evidence must be:
- **Complete** — all IMM-P® stages must include required artifacts  
- **Traceable** — decisions, changes, and iterations must be recorded  
- **Versioned** — repository retains chronological history  
- **Auditable** — evidence must support MEL and accreditation  
- **Accessible** — clearly named, properly stored, searchable  

## 07.3 Evidence Repository Structure
Standard folder hierarchy:

/evidence
/00-pre-discovery
/01-discovery
/02-validation
/03-efficiency
/04-scaling
/05-continuous-improvement
/governance
/risks
/prototypes
/experiments
/maturity
/mel


## 07.4 Metadata Requirements
Every file must include a metadata header:

- Title  
- Author  
- Date  
- IMM-P® Stage  
- Version  
- Evidence Type  
- Related Decision Record(s)  
- Tags  

## 07.5 Decision Record Format
All decisions must be logged using the following fields:

- Decision title  
- Context  
- Evidence used  
- Options considered  
- Final decision  
- Responsible person  
- Date  
- Escalation status  
- Link(s) to related evidence  

## 07.6 Prototyping & Experimentation Logs

### Prototyping Log (Minimum Fields)
- Prototype version  
- Objective  
- Features tested  
- Feedback received  
- Iteration notes  

### Experiment Log (Minimum Fields)
- Hypothesis  
- Method  
- User group  
- Results  
- Interpretation  
- Next steps  

## 07.7 Repository Hygiene Rules
- No duplicate files  
- No orphan evidence (all files must map to a stage)  
- No undocumented decisions  
- No “final_v2_final_really_final” filenames  
- Version numbering: `v1`, `v1.1`, `v2`, etc.  
- Annual evidence consolidation required

# Annex 08 — Risk & Ethics Protocols (IGF-Aligned)

## 08.1 Purpose
This annex defines the risk, ethics, and integrity protocols required for compliance with the Innovation Governance Framework (IGF).  
It ensures that labs and hubs engage in responsible experimentation and uphold system integrity.

## 08.2 Risk Principles
- **Early identification**  
- **Documented assessment**  
- **Proportional mitigation**  
- **Mandatory escalation** when above Tier-0 authority  
- **Transparency** in reporting  

## 08.3 Risk Categories
- Operational  
- Strategic  
- Political  
- Ethical  
- User safety  
- Data protection and privacy  
- Reputation  
- Technical and implementation risk  

## 08.4 Risk Log Template
Fields required:

- Risk description  
- Category  
- Likelihood (Low/Med/High)  
- Impact (Low/Med/High)  
- Mitigation plan  
- Responsible person  
- Escalation status  
- Review date  

## 08.5 Ethics Protocol

### Core Requirements
- Fairness  
- Non-discrimination  
- Transparency  
- Informed participation  
- Safe user testing  
- Privacy compliance  
- Documentation of ethical dilemmas  

### Sensitive Experiments
Experiments involving citizen impact, AI systems, public decisions, or vulnerable populations require:
- Tier-1 review  
- Tier-2 approval  

## 08.6 Integrity Protocol
All labs and hubs must uphold:
- Evidence honesty  
- Documentation integrity  
- Accurate reporting

## 08.7 Escalation Thresholds

| Threshold | Escalate to | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Low | Tier-0 | Document only |

# Annex 15 — Glossary Expansion

## 15.1 Purpose
This annex expands the glossary of VILF 1.0, providing authoritative definitions of all key concepts, aligned with MCF 2.1, IMM, IMM-P®, IGF, and MEL.

## 15.2 Terms

### **VILF — Vigía Innovation Lab Framework**
A structural framework for designing, governing, and scaling innovation lab networks.

### **VXF — Vigía Cross-Framework Architecture**
The shared structural and conceptual spine connecting VILF, VIF, VIGF, and future frameworks.

### **Tier-0 / Tier-1 / Tier-2**
Three-tier governance model:
- Tier-0: Labs  
- Tier-1: Hubs  
- Tier-2: Network Coordination Unit  

### **IMM — Innovation Maturity Model**
Capability and institutional maturity evaluation system.
